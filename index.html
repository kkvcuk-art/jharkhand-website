Jharkhand

The word "Jhar" means '"forest and "Khand" means "land" in various Indo-Aryan languages. Jharkhand known as the "Land of Forests".

Jharkhand is a state in eastern India. Our state, that was born out of Bihar in 2000 , has been gifted with powerful hills, rushing rivers, dense jungles, and a rich tribal culture. Nature and legacy exist in Jharkhand, from the ancient customs of our Adivasi tribes to the successful companies of today. There is something genuinely unique about our region, whether it is our breathtaking waterfalls or the many minerals found beneath the soil.

Featured Attractions

Lodh Falls

Lodh Falls, also known as Burha Ghagh Falls , is the tallest waterfall in Jharkhand, India, with a height of about 143 meters (470 feet). Located in the Latehar district near the Netarhat plateau , it cascades down from the Burha River amidst dense forests and tribal villages.

Parasnath Hills

Parasnath Hill is one of the most important pilgrimage sites for Jains in India. It is located in the Giridih district of Jharkhand, and it is the highest peak in Jharkhand and the Chotanagpur Plateau, with an elevation of 1,365 meters (4,478 feet) .

Rajrappa

Chhinnamastika Temple is a Hindu temple , dedicated to Goddess Chinnamastika is a Hindu pilgrimage centre located in Rajrappa , in Ramgarh district of Jharkhand, India .
