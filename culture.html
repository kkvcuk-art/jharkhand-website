Introduction

Jharkhand is often called the "Land of Tribes", home to communities like Santhal, Munda, Oraon, Ho, and Kharia. Its culture blends tribal traditions and neighboring influences, resulting in a vibrant identity. Festivals, expressive dances, soulful music, and rich handicrafts define its heritage.

Chhau Dance

Semi-classical dance with martial elements; performed with masks and elaborate costumes. Narrates stories from epics.

Santhali Dance

Performed during harvest festivals; men and women dance in circles to drums and flutes.

Karma Dance

Celebrates fertility and prosperity around the sacred Karma tree.

Paika Dance

Warrior dance showcasing bravery with swords and shields.

Nagpuri Dance

Popular among non-tribal communities; performed during social gatherings and festivals.

Sohrai & Kohbar Paintings

Mural paintings using natural colors. Sohrai depicts animals and farming life; Kohbar is for marriages.

Paitkar Paintings

Scroll paintings narrating stories through hand-drawn illustrations.

Bamboo & Wood Crafts

Household items, baskets, and decorative pieces.

Dokra Art

Lost-wax metal casting used for jewelry, idols, and figurines.

Tribal Festivals

  • Sarhul: Spring festival; worships the Sal tree for a good harvest.
  • Sohrai: Post-harvest festival honoring cattle.
  • Karma: Singing and dancing around the Karma tree.
  • Tusu Parab: Winter harvest festival; folk songs honor Goddess Tusu.
